## About the Project

This repository contains the source code for a public research portfolio documenting a Master's capstone project investigating how AI adoption for workflow optimization can enhance the economic resilience of immigrant-owned micro and small businesses — and under what conditions those gains scale into broader urban resilience.

The research is conducted through the New York State Entrepreneurship Assistance Centers (EAC) Program with [Welcome to Chinatown](https://welcometochinatown.com/), a nonprofit supporting small businesses in NYC's Chinatown. It draws on structured needs assessments and survey data from 537 small business owners across 25 EACs, surveyed in English, Spanish, and Chinese.

The portfolio includes an executive summary, interactive theory of change, full curriculum scope and sequence, survey findings, and chapter blueprint — built as a living document that grows alongside the research.

## Research at a Glance

- **Central question:** How can AI adoption for workflow optimization be designed and implemented to enhance the economic resilience of immigrant-owned micro and small businesses, and under what conditions can these gains scale into broader urban resilience?
- **Site of inquiry:** NYS EAC Program with Welcome to Chinatown, New York City
- **Survey reach:** 537 SMB respondents · 25 EACs · 3 languages (EN, ES, ZH)
- **Curriculum:** 6-module AI training program grounded in Kolb's Experiential Learning Cycle and Fink's Taxonomy of Significant Learning
- **Submission:** May 2026 · Bard College / Central European University
